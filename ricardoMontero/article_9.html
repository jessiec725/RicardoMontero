Teen Pregnancy Higher in Religious States

December 12, 2016 by Ricardo Montero

Ricardo Montero

In states where the population of Christians is over 70 percent, and where 70 percent or more of the adults say that religion is very important, the pregnancy rates of people between 15-19 years-old are the highest in the nation. Alabama, Arkansas, Kentucky, Louisiana, Mississippi, New Mexico, Oklahoma, Texas, Tennessee, West Virginia and Wyoming, states with large religious populations, all have at least 30 teen pregnancies per 1000 people; Arkansas has the highest rate with 39.9 pregnancies.

Coincidentally, sex-education is not mandated in any of those states except for Kentucky, Mississippi and New Mexico, but the information does not need to be medically accurate in any of the states. The states with the lowest teen pregnancy rates all have Christian populations of 70 percent or less and 70 percent or less of adults think that religion is very important. They are also mostly in the Northeast. Sixteen states in total have less than 20 teen pregnancies per 1000 people; Massachusetts has the lowest with 10.6 pregnancies.

However, lack of medically accurate sex-ed or lack of sex-ed at all, is not just limited to the religious states. According to the National Conference of State Legislations, only 24 states and the District of Columbia require public schools teach sex education. Some of the states with the lowest pregnancy rates do not teach sex-ed, such as New Hampshire, New York and Pennsylvania.
